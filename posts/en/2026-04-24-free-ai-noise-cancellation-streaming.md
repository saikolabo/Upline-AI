You're streaming from your living room, your roommate's watching TV in the next room, and your mic is picking up absolutely everything. The result: your viewers hear more background noise than your actual voice. Good news: artificial intelligence can fix this problem in just a few clicks — and often completely free. In this article, we break down the best solutions to **remove background noise from your mic using AI at no cost**, whether you're a streamer, gamer, or content creator.

---

## Why AI Is Changing the Game for Noise Removal

For a long time, filtering out background noise meant either buying a high-end $200 microphone or wrestling with complex audio processing in a DAW. AI has changed everything. Trained on thousands of hours of audio, these tools can distinguish your voice from virtually any unwanted noise: PC fans, mechanical keyboard clicks, barking dogs, or street noise.

The concept is straightforward: an algorithm analyzes your audio stream in real time, identifies unwanted frequencies, and removes them without affecting your voice — all with near-zero latency, which is essential for live streaming or real-time calls.

---

## Krisp: The Go-To Option, Even on the Free Tier

Krisp is arguably the best-known tool in this space. It works elegantly: it installs as a virtual audio driver on your PC or Mac. You select it as your mic input in OBS, Discord, or any other application, and it filters noise on the fly.

The free version offers **60 minutes of filtering per day**, which can be plenty for shorter sessions. It includes suppression of ambient noise, background voices, and even echo cancellation. The results are impressive — even with a budget microphone.

If you regularly stream for more than an hour, you'll want to consider the paid plan. But for testing the technology or occasional use cases like meetings or short podcast recordings, [Krisp](https://krisp.pxf.io/YV16zJ) is clearly the ideal starting point.

---

## NVIDIA Broadcast: Free If You Have the Right GPU

If your PC runs an NVIDIA RTX graphics card, you already have access to one of the best noise suppression tools available — and it's **completely free**. [NVIDIA Broadcast](https://www.nvidia.com/broadcast) leverages your GPU's Tensor Cores to process audio (and video) in real time with serious computational power.

The noise reduction feature works the same way as Krisp: a virtual microphone appears in your system, and you select it in your apps. Beyond mic noise suppression, NVIDIA Broadcast also offers:

- **Ambient noise suppression** for both input and output (so you won't hear your colleagues' keyboards during calls)
- **Background replacement** for your webcam
- **Auto-framing** for your camera

For PC streamers who already own an RTX 2060 or better, this is the zero-compromise solution. No subscription, no time limits. The only catch: it requires an NVIDIA RTX GPU, which rules out AMD users and laptop setups without a dedicated card.

---

## Cleanvoice AI: Perfect for Podcasters and Content Creators

[Cleanvoice AI](https://cleanvoice.ai/) takes a different approach from the two tools above: it doesn't work in real time, but rather in **post-production**. You upload your audio file, and the AI automatically removes background noise, filler words like "uh" and "um," mouth clicks, and awkward silences.

It's the ideal tool if you record podcasts, voiceovers, or video tutorials. The results are remarkable: in just a few minutes, a recording captured in a noisy room sounds clean and professional. The free version includes a few minutes of processed audio so you can test the service before committing — more than enough to judge the quality.

For a streamer who repurposes their sessions or a YouTuber editing their videos, Cleanvoice AI saves a significant amount of time compared to manual processing in Audacity or Adobe Audition.

## How to Choose the Right Solution for Your Needs

Here's a quick breakdown to help you decide:

| Profile | Recommended Solution |
|---|---|
| Streamer with NVIDIA RTX GPU | NVIDIA Broadcast (free, unlimited) |
| Streamer without RTX / Mac / Linux | Krisp (free 60 min/day) |
| Podcaster / video creator | Cleanvoice AI (post-production) |
| Mixed streaming + podcast use | Krisp + Cleanvoice AI |

One practical tip: don't overlook the basics. Even the best AI tool won't make up for a mic sitting 50 cm from your mouth. Position your microphone 10–15 cm away, use a pop filter if you have one, and let the AI handle the rest.
