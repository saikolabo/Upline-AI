A clacking keyboard, a humming fan, a noisy neighbor — your microphone picks up everything, and your audience hears every bit of it. The good news: in 2026, removing background noise with AI is within everyone's reach, and several powerful tools are available completely free of charge. Whether you're a streamer, gamer, podcaster, or just working from home, this roundup will transform your audio quality without costing you a dime.

## Why AI Is Revolutionizing Background Noise Removal

For a long time, getting clean audio meant spending $200 on a microphone or wrestling with complex DAW processing. Artificial intelligence has changed everything. Modern algorithms analyze your audio stream in real time, distinguish the human voice from unwanted noise, and filter it out with remarkable accuracy.

The result: even with a budget microphone, you can sound like a professional. These solutions work by sitting between your physical microphone and your applications (Discord, OBS, Zoom, etc.), creating a clean virtual microphone that your software uses instead.

## Krisp: The Swiss Army Knife of Noise Suppression

[Krisp](https://krisp.pxf.io/YV16zJ) is arguably the gold standard in this space. Its AI algorithm filters ambient noise on both ends of the conversation — your side AND the other person's. That's a major advantage for professional calls or team gaming sessions.

**What the free tier includes:**
- 60 minutes of filtering per week
- Noise, echo, and background sound removal
- Compatible with all apps (Discord, Teams, OBS, Zoom…)
- Available on Windows and macOS

The 60-minute weekly limit is fine for occasional use, but streamers broadcasting for several hours a day will need to consider the Pro plan. What's impressive is that even the free tier holds its own against paid competing solutions. Setup is quick, the interface is intuitive, and the results are immediately noticeable.

## NVIDIA Broadcast: The Ultimate Tool for RTX Users

If you have an NVIDIA RTX graphics card in your setup, you already have access to one of the best noise suppression tools available — and it's **completely free**. [NVIDIA Broadcast](https://www.nvidia.com/broadcast) leverages the Tensor Cores in your GPU to perform real-time AI processing without impacting your gaming performance.

**Key features:**
- Real-time microphone noise suppression
- Echo cancellation
- Camera enhancements (virtual background, auto-framing, eye contact correction)
- No time limits whatsoever

The audio filter quality is genuinely impressive. Try running a vacuum cleaner in the background — NVIDIA Broadcast nearly eliminates it entirely. The software integrates seamlessly into OBS workflows and works with any audio application.

The one catch — and it's a deal-breaker for some — is that you absolutely need an RTX 2060 or higher. If your GPU doesn't meet that requirement, this option simply isn't available to you.

## Cleanvoice AI: Perfect for Podcasters and Content Creators

[Cleanvoice AI](https://cleanvoice.ai/) takes a different approach from the two tools above. Rather than working in real time, it processes your audio files in post-production. This makes it ideal for podcasters, YouTubers, and creators who record their content before publishing it.

**What it can do:**
- Remove mouth noises (smacks, audible breaths)
- Reduce background noise
- Detect and cut unnecessarily long silences
- Automatic processing in just a few minutes

The free version lets you test the service with short files. The processing quality is excellent, particularly for mouth noises that real-time tools tend to handle less effectively. If your workflow includes an editing phase, Cleanvoice AI is a powerful complement to the other solutions mentioned here.

## How to Choose the Right Tool for Your Needs

With these three tools in mind, here's how to narrow down your choice:

| Situation | Recommended Solution |
|---|---|
| Live streaming/gaming, RTX GPU | NVIDIA Broadcast |
| Calls, Discord, remote work | Krisp (free tier) |
| Podcast, YouTube, post-production | Cleanvoice AI |
| No NVIDIA GPU, varied use cases | Krisp |

**A few practical tips to get the best results:**

1. **Position your microphone correctly.** AI can only do so much — a mic placed too far away picks up more room sound and makes the job harder. Aim for 6 to 8 inches from your mouth.
2. **Stack your solutions.** You can run Krisp alongside OBS's built-in noise suppression filter for a two-layer approach.
3. **Test before your sessions.** Record 30 seconds and listen back. Some settings can be adjusted depending on your environment.
4. **Avoid over-filtering.** Too much suppression can make your voice sound robotic. Find the sweet spot between clarity and naturalness.
