Krisp has long been the go-to solution for real-time background noise removal, but its monthly subscription can quickly add up. The good news is that in 2026, several free alternatives are giving it a serious run for its money. Whether you're a streamer, podcaster, or remote worker, there are AI-powered tools capable of cutting out unwanted noise without costing you a dime. In this article, we break down the best options available, their strengths, their limitations, and most importantly, how to pick the right one for your setup.

## Why Look for a Free Krisp Alternative?

Krisp does offer a free tier, but it's capped at **60 minutes of filtering per week** — nowhere near enough for a streamer or a professional who spends their days on video calls. The Pro subscription runs around $8 per month, which makes sense for heavy users, but is hard to justify for occasional use.

The free alternatives that have emerged in recent years fill this gap with AI-based noise suppression technology, often built directly into existing software or available as standalone downloads. Some offload the processing to your GPU, others run entirely in the cloud, and a few even work offline with no time limits whatsoever.

## NVIDIA Broadcast: The Best Option If You Have an NVIDIA GPU

If your PC is running an NVIDIA graphics card (RTX 2060 or higher), [NVIDIA Broadcast](https://www.nvidia.com/broadcast) is hands down **the best free Krisp alternative** available. The software uses AI processed directly on your GPU to suppress background noise, eliminate echo, and even enhance your webcam's video quality in real time.

**What it does well:**
- Highly effective background noise removal, even in loud environments
- Built-in echo cancellation, ideal for acoustically untreated rooms
- Completely free, with no time limits or feature restrictions
- Integrates as a virtual device in OBS, Discord, Zoom, Teams, and more

**Its limitations:**
- Exclusive to NVIDIA RTX GPU owners — no equivalent for AMD or Intel users
- May put a slight strain on performance during intensive gaming sessions

For PC streamers and gamers running NVIDIA hardware, this one is simply unbeatable for the price — which is free.

## Cleanvoice AI: The Go-To for Podcasting and Audio Recording

If you're after a **post-production audio** solution rather than real-time processing, [Cleanvoice AI](https://cleanvoice.ai/) is well worth your attention. This web-based tool analyzes your recordings and automatically removes background noise, filler words, lip smacks, and unwanted silences.

It's not a real-time solution like Krisp, but for podcasters and content creators who record first and edit later, it's a massive time-saver. The free tier comes with enough starting credits to properly test its processing quality on your own audio files.

**Strengths:**
- Highly precise audio cleanup, especially on voice recordings
- Simple interface, no technical expertise required
- Professional-grade results on indoor recordings
- Compatible with MP3, WAV, and files exported from major DAWs

**Worth noting:** Cleanvoice AI complements rather than directly replaces Krisp. If you produce podcasts or voiceovers, it's a tool worth having in your arsenal — even alongside a real-time solution.

## Built-In Options Already in Your Existing Software

Before installing a third-party tool, it's worth checking what your current software already offers out of the box:

- **Discord** has included an AI-powered noise suppression filter for several versions now, toggleable with one click in your voice settings. It's free and more than adequate for gaming communication.
- **OBS Studio** with the **NVIDIA RTX Voice** plugin or the **RNNoise** filter provides respectable noise reduction directly in your audio chain, with no extra software needed.
- **Google Meet and Microsoft Teams** both offer their own AI noise suppression, which is sufficient for the vast majority of professional meetings.

These built-in solutions don't always match Krisp's precision, but for standard professional use, they get the job done without spending a cent.

## How to Choose Based on Your Profile

Here's a quick breakdown to point you in the right direction based on your use case:

| Profile | Recommended Solution |
|---|---|
| Streamer with NVIDIA GPU | NVIDIA Broadcast (free, unlimited) |
| Podcaster / video creator | Cleanvoice AI (post-production) |
| Remote worker on video calls | Native Discord / Teams / Meet filters |
| AMD/Intel user without a dedicated GPU | RNNoise via OBS or SteelSeries Sonar |

The key is figuring out whether you need **real-time** processing (during a stream or call) or **after-the-fact** cleanup (to polish a recording). These two use cases call for different tools, and most free alternatives specialize in one or the other.
