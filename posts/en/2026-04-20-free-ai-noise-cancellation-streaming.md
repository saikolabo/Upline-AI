Is your mic picking up keyboard clatter, your PC fan, or street noise from outside? Whether you're a streamer, a gamer, or working from home, poor audio quality instantly kills your credibility. The good news: artificial intelligence can remove background noise in real time, and several powerful solutions are available completely free. Here's a full breakdown of the tools you absolutely need to know about in 2026.

## Why AI Is Changing the Game for Noise Removal

Traditional noise filters worked through thresholding — they simply cut any frequencies below a certain volume level. The result? A robotic-sounding voice, jarring cutoffs, and an unnatural audio experience.

AI-based solutions work differently. They've been trained on millions of hours of audio to **learn how to distinguish a human voice from any unwanted noise**. The results are genuinely impressive: your voice stays clear, warm, and natural, even in a noisy environment. Latency has also been cut to a minimum, making these tools perfectly usable in a live stream or voice call.

## Krisp: The Go-To Free Option for Everyone

[Krisp](https://krisp.pxf.io/YV16zJ) has established itself as the number one AI noise removal solution — and for good reason. The concept is straightforward: the app creates virtual microphone and speaker devices on your system. You select these in Discord, OBS, Teams, or any other application, and Krisp filters noise in real time before your voice ever reaches the other person.

**What the free plan includes:**
- 60 minutes of noise suppression per week
- Noise filtering on both input (your mic) and output (other participants)
- Compatible with all audio apps, no complex setup required

The 60-minute weekly cap might seem tight for an active streamer, but it's more than enough to test the quality and decide whether upgrading is worth it. For occasional meetings or casual gaming sessions, it's a genuinely fair offer.

## NVIDIA Broadcast: Maximum Power for RTX Users

If your setup includes an NVIDIA RTX graphics card, [NVIDIA Broadcast](https://www.nvidia.com/broadcast) is arguably the best tool available to you — and it's completely free with no time restrictions.

The app leverages your GPU's Tensor Cores to handle noise suppression locally on your machine. That brings two major advantages: near-zero latency and no audio data sent to remote servers. For streamers who care about privacy or broadcast sensitive content, that's a significant plus.

Beyond the microphone, NVIDIA Broadcast also offers:
- Noise suppression on incoming audio (filtering the voices you hear)
- Virtual backgrounds for your webcam without a green screen
- Automatic face tracking via your camera

**The only catch**: you need an RTX card (RTX 2060 or better recommended). If your rig is already equipped, look no further for free AI-powered mic noise removal.

## Cleanvoice AI: A Must-Have for Podcasters and Content Creators

[Cleanvoice AI](https://cleanvoice.ai/) takes a different approach from the previous two tools: rather than filtering in real time, it processes your audio files after recording. That makes it the ideal solution if you produce pre-recorded content — podcasts, YouTube voiceovers, video tutorials.

Its AI automatically detects and removes:
- Persistent background noise
- Filler words like "uh," "um," and other verbal hesitations
- Lip smacks and mouth noises
- Excessively long silences

The free plan allows up to 30 minutes of audio processing per month — enough to test the quality on your own recordings. For creators publishing regularly, the paid plan quickly pays for itself in time saved during post-production.

## How to Choose Based on Your Use Case

The right tool depends entirely on what you're doing:

**Streaming or playing online?** Go with NVIDIA Broadcast if you have an RTX card, or Krisp for a universal solution with no hardware requirements. Both work in real time and integrate into OBS Studio, Discord, or TeamSpeak in just a few clicks.

**Creating pre-recorded content?** Cleanvoice AI is your best bet. No need to invest in a $300 studio microphone when an algorithm can clean up your recording in seconds.

**Working from home?** Krisp was built exactly for this. Its integration with Teams, Zoom, and Google Meet is native, and the free plan covers occasional meetings with ease.

Worth noting: these tools aren't mutually exclusive. Many streamers use NVIDIA Broadcast for live sessions and Cleanvoice AI to polish their VODs or clips before uploading.
